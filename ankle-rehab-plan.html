Weekly measurement Knee-to-wall · weight-bearing lunge

  1. Barefoot. Tape measure fixed to the floor, perpendicular to the wall.
  2. Second toe and heel centre aligned along the tape.
  3. Knee tracks over the second toe and touches the wall. Heel must stay down.
  4. Record the longest distance where both conditions hold.
  5. 3 attempts — record the best.
  6. Same conditions every time. Unwarmed, pre-session is the more honest number.
  7. Always measure both ankles.
One-off diagnostic. Measure knee straight-ish vs. deeply bent. Improves a lot with the knee bent → restriction is more calf / soleus (muscular). Same either way, with a hard pinching block at the front → points to joint / capsular or bony restriction — this won't respond to mobility work, and is the reason for the 6-week checkpoint.

Progression rules make "challenging" operational

  • End each strength set with 1–2 reps in reserve — certain you couldn't do 4 more.
  • Add load when you hit the top of the rep range with good form on all sets.
  • Calf-raise standard: full heel drop, no knee bounce, no trunk lean.
  • After the glides, spend 2–3 min loading the new range — deep split-squat holds, slow lunges to the wall. Range you don't load, you don't keep.
Calf-raise capacity benchmark 24 reps ♂ · 21 reps ♀ Median single-leg heel rises to failure on a 10° incline (566 healthy adults). Test both legs every 4 weeks — the injured / uninjured ratio beats the raw number. Target >90%.

The first two together are the classic pattern for anterior ankle impingement or a capsular restriction — neither responds well to more of the same exercises. That is precisely why the checkpoint exists.
On the glides
Load the range you just opened. Weight-bearing mobilisation-with-movement opens a window rather than making lasting change — the effect is largely acute. Don't finish on the glide; load into the new range afterward. Manage expectations: the technique targets excessive anterior talus translation after a lateral sprain; its value months into rehab is less established. Low-risk and worth 5 minutes, but unlikely to unlock the range on its own.
Note on interpretation
"Glide" is read as a talocrural mobilisation-with-movement (posterior talus / anterior tibia glide while lunging into dorsiflexion). "Resisted ankle reflections" is read as resisted eversion — eversion strength matters most after a lateral ligament injury. Worth confirming with the physio.
Uncertainties
Injury grade, ligaments involved, and any residual laxity are unknown here; baseline numbers are unknown; the rep and load ranges are conventional but generic. The physio's judgement overrides anything in this document.
